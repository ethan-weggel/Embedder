Wheat farming is one of the most significant agricultural activities worldwide, providing a staple crop for millions of people. This resilient cereal grain is grown in a range of climates, though it thrives in temperate zones with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall. Wheat varieties are selected based on desired characteristics such as resistance to pests and diseases, yield potential, and grain quality. Modern wheat farming also uses precision agriculture techniques to monitor crop health and optimize water, fertilizer, and pesticide use, which enhances yield while minimizing environmental impact. Once harvested, wheat is processed into flour, which forms the basis of numerous food products globally, making it a crucial part of food security and economic stability.
